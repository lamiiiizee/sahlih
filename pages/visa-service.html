Tourist visa

If you have a Tourist Visa worldwide, your spouse usually won't have the right to work in the respective country. Tourist visas are typically for leisure purposes, and employment is generally not permitted. Dependent children, however, may still have access to free education, subject to the rules of the country you're visiting.

Documents Required for an
International Visit or Tourist Visa Application
  • Online completion of the tourist visa application form specific to the destination.
  • Two recent color photographs meeting the guidelines of the respective country, taken within the last six months.
  • Valid passport with at least one blank page, ensuring its validity extends for at least three months beyond your planned departure date from the destination.
  • Proof of Financial Capability:Evidence of financial means adequate to cover living expenses during your stay. This may include the last six months' bank statements or payslips. Information regarding your accommodation in the destination is required, and you are not obligated to make full payment before obtaining the visa. Additionally, this could involve:
    • ID information and residential address of a person covering your travel expenses.
    • ID information and passport number of any relative residing in the destination.
  • Complete travel itinerary:specifying dates of arrival and departure. Provide a detailed plan outlining your activities, such as travel dates, places to visit, scheduled meetings, booked tours, and any planned attendance at festivals.
  • Previous travel history covering the last 10 years, supported by visas, entry/exit stamps in current and previous passports, or separate residence titles
  • Details about your current residential address, along with the duration of your residence there
  • Biometric information: If the visa application is for a stay exceeding 6 months, submission of biometric data is required, including fingerprints and a digital photograph taken at the designated application center.
  • Letter of Invitation: An invitation letter, written by a citizen or legal resident of the destination, extending an invitation to a foreigner with whom they share family or friendship ties. The letter confirms the host's willingness to accommodate the guest throughout their stay in the destination.
  • Payment of applicable visa fees, with a presentation of the receipt as confirmation of payment.

Dependent visa

If you hold an International Work Visa, your spouse will be entitled to live and work in the respective country. They will have the freedom to pursue any employment opportunities available. Additionally, your dependent children will be eligible for free education in that country.

Documents necessary for a dependent visa application include:
  • Passport
  • Tuberculosis test report (if applicable based on nationality)
  • Police clearance check from all countries (for stays exceeding 1 year)
  • Birth Certificate (for children)
  • Marriage Certificate (for married partners)
  • Proof of accommodation in the country (Tenancy Agreement)
  • Employment confirmation letter from your employer or Certificate of Sponsorship (COS)

Your employer, a recognized sponsor, will vouch for the financial support required for your dependents. Consequently, you won't need a personal savings statement. It's important to note that there is no access to public funds for your dependents, and it is your responsibility to cover the expenses associated with their stay in the country.

If you have a specific country or region in mind, it's best to inquire about the particular visa options and requirements for that location.

Employment visa

An international destination stands out as a favored choice for individuals seeking to relocate, pursue employment opportunities, and establish residence in a foreign country. In numerous instances, applicants are required to adhere to the tier points-based visa system of the respective country, a mechanism designed to evaluate eligibility for work or study visas. The majority of visa applications related to employment, exemplified by schemes akin to Tier 2, and educational pursuits under Tier 4, are now processed through this comprehensive system.
